# Summary

## High-Level Contribution
Hamilton introduces the hodograph as a velocity-space curve and proves that for inverse-square central attraction the hodograph is a circle (his "Law of the Circular Hodograph"), giving a compact geometric route from force law to Kepler-orbit structure.
## Relevance to This Project
This is a high-value source for the manuscript's forward-direction discussion ($1/r^2$ force to conic orbit): it supplies a geometric-kinematic mechanism (velocity-circle representation) that complements Principia-style position-space constructions, including a clear angular-momentum-compatible interpretation.
## Notes / Evidence
- Scope: Deepened from primary text inspection (TCD/Wilkins PDF edition of Proceedings RIA vol. 3, pp. 344-353).
- Method: Focused on Hamilton's chain from general hodograph definition to inverse-square circular hodograph and conic-orbit consequences.
- Key claim(s): For general motion, the hodograph tangent gives force direction and its element/time gives force magnitude (p. 1 in PDF text stream).
- Key claim(s): Under central force, orbit and hodograph are linked by a constant-area reciprocal parallelogram relation (pp. 1-2), enabling transfer of geometry between curves.
- Key claim(s): For inverse-square force, hodograph curvature is constant; therefore hodograph is a circle, and Hamilton states the reciprocal characterization (pp. 2-3).
- Key claim(s): Hamilton states a geometric correspondence between orbital angular position and motion on the hodograph circle (p. 3); this is a shape/angle mapping and does not imply constant speed in time along the hodograph.
- Key claim(s): He derives conic-section orbits with the force center as focus and gives semilatus-rectum and eccentricity relations from hodographic geometry (p. 4), then explicitly notes agreement with Newton's derivation of Kepler laws.
- Angular-momentum account (modern notation): with specific angular momentum $h=r^{2}\dot{\theta}$ (constant for central force), the hodograph angular rate tracks orbital angle rate, so $\dot{\theta}_{h}=\dot{\theta}=h/r^{2}$.
- Consequence for hodograph areal speed: for circular hodograph radius $\rho$ (inverse-square case), $\dot{A}_{h}=\frac{1}{2}\rho^{2}\dot{\theta}_{h}\propto 1/r^{2}$; therefore hodograph areal speed is generally not constant in time.
- Interpretation: the conserved quantity is physical orbital angular momentum $h$ in real space; hodograph area sweep encodes instantaneous radial dependence rather than a new constant of motion.
