# Summary

## High-Level Contribution
Maxwell gives a compact pedagogical synthesis of Kepler dynamics where Hamilton's hodograph construction is used to recover the central-force interpretation and inverse-square scaling with clear geometric steps.
## Relevance to This Project
This source is especially valuable for positioning the manuscript between Newton's synthetic geometry and later 19th-century mechanics pedagogy: Maxwell operationalizes the hodograph as a bridge from orbital geometry to force law.
## Notes / Evidence
- Scope: Deepened from direct reading of the Internet Archive text stream of *Matter and Motion* (chapter sequence around Arts. 128-137).
- Method: Tracked Maxwell's progression from Kepler's laws to Newtonian interpretation, with emphasis on Arts. 132-137.
- Key claim(s): Art. 132 explicitly identifies Hamilton's hodograph as the method used to connect orbital motion and velocity geometry.
- Key claim(s): Arts. 133-134 show geometric relations in the ellipse that make a rotated velocity vector trace a circle (center at the force point), giving a constructive hodograph for Kepler motion.
- Key claim(s): Art. 135 converts velocity-space increments to acceleration direction/magnitude and obtains centripetal acceleration proportional to $1/r^2$.
- Key claim(s): Art. 136 combines this with mass scaling to write force between bodies in inverse-square form.
- Key claim(s): Art. 137 discusses Kepler's third law with finite planetary mass correction ($S+P$), clarifying limits of the simplified proportional form.
